My Education

I attended the Math and Science specialty center at Clover Hill High School in Midlothian, Virginia. After high school, I was accepted into the Honors College at Virginia Commonwealth University (VCU), and began my studies in Fall 2023.

In high school, I was the founder and president of our Technology-Student Association, and I spent several club meetings working with other members to help strengthen their programming and design abilities.

At VCU, I was certain of my major immediately, and enrolled in the College of Engineering as a mechanical engineer. It combined my love for invention and design with complex problem solving, which felt like a perfect application of the tools I had learned through my personal work. My coursework also allowed me to design more complex and useful mechanisms than I had ever been able to and reinforced the joy I found in building something new. On top of my major, I also added a nuclear engineering concentration, as well as an aerospace minor. I hope to come back to education later in my life to enroll in a doctorate program for engineering. I've had a great experience with undergraduate research at VCU and I would love to continue onto a more ambitious project in the future, once I've established myself.

After beginning college, I joined a handful of student organizations and found my place within them. I was a part of the Mechanical Team for Hyperlabs, a team that competes in the Intelligent Ground Vehicles Competition for autonomously-navigating robots. I also joined the Game Creator's Coalition at VCU, a hub for game developers to show off their work, offer programming and design advice, and develop ideas. I'm also a member of the American Society of Mechanical Engineers (ASME) and the American Nuclear Society at VCU.

I have a 3.8 GPA, and I have been on the Dean's List since Fall 2023. I've also taken several fundamental and more advanced engineering courses, the most relevant being: Circuits (EGRE 206), CAE Design (EGMN 420), Multivariable Calculus (MATH 307), Computational Methods (EGMN 210), Differential Equations (MATH 301), Fluid Mechanics (EGMN 301) Project Management (SCMA 350), Heat Transfer (EGMN 302), Thermodynamics (EGMN 204)
