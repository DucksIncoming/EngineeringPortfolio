My Experience

Jefferson Lab Particle Accelerator

Position: Engineering Intern

Dates: Jun 2022 - Jul 2022

Job Description: At the Jefferson Lab (JLAB), I worked with an on-site facilities engineer in coordination with Honeywell, a major controls subcontractor working with the lab, to create an enhanced monitoring model for the accelerator's maintenance systems. Our team sought data from JLAB's various sensors as parameters to predict system failures in order to alert onsite engineers before the fault became catastrophic and required shutdown of the accelerator.

We formulated a report for Honeywell regarding the specific sensors that would have the highest success rate in predicting failures, as well as designing a number of potential failure scenarios and the system rules that could be applied using the enhanced monitoring system to prevent catastrophic failure of the accelerator. I gathered an immense amount of experience throughout this internship regarding system planning, maintenance, and the numerous ancillary factors that must be taken into account when designing energy-intensive infrastructure.

Relevant Experience: Reading/creating system schematics and control diagrams, analyzing equipment for points of failure, disseminating relevant findings to adjacent groups, coordinating/communicating across departments.
