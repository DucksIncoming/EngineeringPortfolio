Windchill

Background

Windchill is a game I created as a solo-developer over the course of 8 months, through 2023 and 2024. It's a horror game which draws the player into a mysterious, abandoned cabin, where unexplainable events start to reveal themselves. I was responsible for creating 100% of the code, 3D models, controls and UI, and I created a significant share of of the textures used in the game. I also created some music and sound effects for the game, and the rest of the textures and audio files are open-source and taken from the web.

Game development is a unique kind of project to take on, because it demands skill and experience across a wide variety of disciplines, requiring solo developers to become a "jack of all trades" of sorts. My existing knowledge of 3D modeling through years of using Blender allowed me to create the 3D models used in the game, and my experience using SolidWorks helped to solidfy my understanding of topology and efficient design. My programming abilities were also pushed to their limits, and I spent many long nights trying to overcome problems that at the time seemed unsolvable.

Beyond this, I also had to consider the experience of the players who would be interacting with the game. I ran several playtests with friends and colleagues at VCU, and I received a tremendous amount of valuable feedback. Ultimately I was able to execute on my vision of developing a cohesive horror experience, and I was extremely happy with how the game plays as a whole.
